Staff Information

How it works

If customers want photos then I will follow their dive group for one dive. It is not possible to guarantee results for more than 1 group per dive, so the price is per group diving together. If People that are booking together are not diving together then they will need to book me to cover more than one dive. The second (and third) can be discounted. Details down the page.

I will try to get shots of the customers entering the water, demonstrating skills, exploring and observing some of the creatures. CESA and safety stops are often good photo ops. I can usually get some good portraits on the surface at the end of a dive.

Guidelines

  • Please give me plenty warning before you get in the water so I can be ready to catch some entry shots.
  • During the dive you can continue as you would if I was not there. I'm self reliant trained, so if we lose contact do not stop to look for me. Your focus is your students or customers.
  • Don't forget to look at the camera occasionally but otherwise, ignore me.
  • Bear in mind the no interference rules. It's good to get pictures with the wildlife but that doesn't mean handling it, or stressing it out in any way.
  • Don't be in too much of a rush to get out of the water. Divers are usually happy at the end of a dive, and I can often get great shots then. These are good for advertising. Smiling diver, {{divecentre}} boat in the background...
  • Although I will not normally assist with new divers in the water (because to do so means I cannot take photos) I am fully trained and can help out in an an emergency. Once the camera is safely stowed, I'm happy to assist with things like CESA line recovery.

Benefits

Firstly you get a commission. This is currently {{commission}}% of the photo selling price.

Secondly you are probably going to get some good pictures out of this, although you may need to be a little patient. Although I try to get the customer pics sorted by the next day, sometimes when I'm busy I will skip the staff pics. Remind me if you haven't seen anything within a week.

You get basically the same conditions as the customers. You can share them online wherever you like as long as they:

  • are not edited (including cropping). get in touch if you want to edit. I'm probably going to say yes, but ask anyway.
